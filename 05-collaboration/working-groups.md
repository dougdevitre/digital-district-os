# Working Groups

## Structure

Three standing working groups support the Digital District Authority Board. Each has a clear charter, cadence, and deliverables.

---

## Tech Working Group

**Charter:** Evaluate, recommend, and guide technical decisions across all four stack layers.

**Membership:** 5-9 members
- Infrastructure engineers (cloud, networking)
- Application developers
- AI/ML practitioners
- Security specialist
- Open-source community representative

**Cadence:** Biweekly, 60 minutes, virtual

**Standing Agenda:**
1. Infrastructure health review (5 min)
2. Active technical decisions requiring input (20 min)
3. Security and compliance updates (10 min)
4. Upcoming technical work preview (15 min)
5. Open discussion (10 min)

**Deliverables:**
- Technology selection recommendations → Board
- API standard updates → Published to repository
- Security audit findings → Board + Operational Team
- Architecture Decision Records (ADRs) for significant choices

**Decision Authority:**
- **Can decide:** Technology selection within approved budget, API standards, coding standards, tool choices
- **Must escalate:** Decisions involving user data policy, vendor contracts > $10K, changes to identity architecture

---

## Policy Working Group

**Charter:** Develop and maintain data governance, privacy, equity, and regulatory compliance policies.

**Membership:** 5-9 members
- Privacy/data governance specialist
- City government representative (legal or policy)
- Community advocate
- Academic researcher (urban policy, digital equity)
- Digital rights representative

**Cadence:** Monthly, 60 minutes, hybrid

**Standing Agenda:**
1. Policy issues surfaced since last meeting (15 min)
2. Active policy drafts — review and feedback (25 min)
3. Regulatory environment updates (10 min)
4. Community feedback on existing policies (10 min)

**Deliverables:**
- Data governance policies → Board for approval, Community Advisory for review
- Privacy impact assessments → Board
- Equity policy recommendations → Board
- Regulatory compliance reports → Board

**Decision Authority:**
- **Can decide:** Policy language and structure, compliance approach, assessment methodology
- **Must escalate:** Any policy affecting user data rights, changes to equity commitments, legal agreements

---

## Community Advisory Board

**Charter:** Represent community interests in all Digital District decisions, with veto power over data governance and equity policies.

**Membership:** 7-12 members
- Residents from target corridors (minimum 3)
- Small business owners from target corridors (minimum 2)
- Community organization leaders (minimum 2)
- Youth representative (1)
- At-large community members

**Selection:** Open application process. Board reviews and selects for geographic, demographic, and sector diversity. Terms are 2 years, staggered. No member serves more than 2 consecutive terms.

**Cadence:** Monthly, 60 minutes, in-person on the corridor (with virtual option)

**Standing Agenda:**
1. Community pulse check — what are you hearing? (15 min)
2. Board decisions requiring community review (20 min)
3. Data governance and equity policy review (15 min)
4. Community priorities for next month (10 min)

**Deliverables:**
- Community feedback reports → Board
- Data policy veto/approval → Board (binding)
- Equity metric review → Board
- Community engagement recommendations → Operational Team

**Special Powers:**
- **Veto authority** on data governance policies, equity metric changes, PII usage, and any decision that could result in displacement or exclusion
- **Priority review** — any Board decision affecting the community must be reviewed by the Advisory Board before finalization
- **Direct escalation** — can bring issues directly to the Board without going through the operational team

**Compensation:** Advisory Board members receive a stipend of $[AMOUNT]/meeting for their time and expertise. Transportation and childcare support provided for in-person meetings.

## Cross-Working-Group Coordination

| When | What | Who |
|------|------|-----|
| Quarterly | All-working-group sync | Chairs of all 3 groups + Board chair |
| As needed | Joint sessions on cross-cutting issues | Relevant members from 2+ groups |
| Annually | Strategy retreat | All groups + Board + operational team |

## Creating New Working Groups

As the Digital District scales, additional working groups may be needed (e.g., Economic Development, Education, Health). New working groups require:
1. A written charter (modeled on the templates above)
2. Board approval
3. At least 5 committed members
4. A clear relationship to an existing gap in governance coverage

## Dissolving Working Groups

A working group can be dissolved if:
1. Its charter has been fully achieved
2. It has fewer than 3 active members for 2 consecutive quarters
3. The Board votes to dissolve (with 30-day notice to members)
