# Glossary

## Core Concepts

**Digital District**
An AI-powered, cloud-native ecosystem that provides entrepreneurs with instant access to tools, infrastructure, and intelligence without requiring physical location. Replaces the legacy Innovation District model.

**Innovation District (Legacy)**
A geographically bounded zone where cities cluster startups, labs, and institutions around anchor tenants (universities, hospitals) through real estate development and public-private partnerships. Based on the GIID model.

**Digital Mainstreet**
A physical corridor (street, transit line, or commercial strip) that has been overlaid with digital infrastructure — community Wi-Fi, real-time data systems, blockchain-enabled economic participation, and streaming platforms. The physical world becomes the substrate for a digital operating system. Reference implementation: Delmar Boulevard, St. Louis.

**Digital Spine**
The underlying technical architecture that connects businesses, services, and residents across a Digital District — blockchain, streaming platforms, AI interfaces, and community Wi-Fi acting as connective tissue.

## Stack Layers

**Layer 1: Infrastructure**
Cloud compute (AWS, Vercel), scalable storage, identity/auth systems. The foundation that everything else runs on.

**Layer 2: Intelligence**
AI models, prompt systems, automation workflows. The decision-support and generation layer that replaces institutional knowledge gatekeepers.

**Layer 3: Applications**
Purpose-built, interconnected business tools — legal tech, financial instruments, marketing engines, communication platforms.

**Layer 4: Distribution**
APIs, global marketplaces, content channels. The mechanisms through which Digital District services reach users worldwide.

## Operational Terms

**Corridor Selection**
The process of choosing which physical street or transit corridor to overlay with digital infrastructure. Factors include existing cultural assets, transit connectivity, socioeconomic diversity, and existing business density.

**Node**
A specialized participant in the Digital District network — can be a physical district (like Cortex), a business, an institution, or an individual entrepreneur. Nodes connect to the network; the network does not depend on any single node.

**System Layer**
The digital infrastructure that sits between physical assets and economic outcomes. Equivalent to middleware in software architecture — it connects what exists below (buildings, streets, institutions) to what's produced above (economic inclusion, entrepreneurial output).

**Readiness Score**
A diagnostic rating (0-40 scale) that measures a city or corridor's preparedness to deploy Digital District infrastructure across eight dimensions.

## Comparison Terms

**Place-Based Innovation**
Economic development strategy that depends on physical proximity and geographic clustering. The old model.

**Platform-Based Innovation**
Economic development strategy that depends on digital access, AI tools, and cloud infrastructure. The new model.

**Buildings vs. Bandwidth**
The central funding allocation question: should public capital flow to physical real estate and construction, or to digital infrastructure (cloud credits, open APIs, universal AI access, community connectivity)?

**Linear Growth vs. Exponential Growth**
Innovation Districts grow linearly — constrained by physical space, construction timelines, and institutional speed. Digital Districts grow exponentially — enabled by cloud scalability, AI-powered automation, and universal access.

## St. Louis–Specific Terms

**Cortex Innovation Community**
St. Louis's flagship Innovation District in midtown. $1.3B+ invested in physical real estate development. Anchored by Washington University, BJC HealthCare, and Saint Louis University. Successful at clustering biotech and tech companies but geographically constrained.

**39 North**
St. Louis's AgTech Innovation District in Creve Coeur/Olivette area. Focused on agricultural technology and plant sciences. Anchored by the Danforth Plant Science Center.

**Downtown North (DTNO)**
Geospatial intelligence district in north downtown St. Louis. Anchored by the National Geospatial-Intelligence Agency (NGA) campus.

**Delmar Boulevard / The Loop**
Cultural and commercial corridor running east-west through the center of St. Louis. Known as a socioeconomic dividing line (the "Delmar Divide"). Proposed site for America's first Digital Mainstreet — overlaying digital infrastructure on existing cultural assets to bridge the divide through connectivity rather than construction.

**The Delmar Model**
The specific implementation pattern of a Digital Mainstreet on Delmar Boulevard — community Wi-Fi, real-time data infrastructure, blockchain-enabled economic participation, and streaming platforms layered over the physical street.
