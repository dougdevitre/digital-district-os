# Stakeholder Audiences

## Audience Map

| Audience | What They Care About | Decision Power | Key Objection | Entry Point |
|----------|---------------------|---------------|---------------|-------------|
| **Mayor / City Executive** | Economic development outcomes, equity, national recognition | Approve/block funding | "We already invested $1B+ in Cortex" | `government-pitch.md` |
| **City Council** | Constituent impact, job creation, cost vs. benefit | Approve budgets, pass ordinances | "How does this help my ward?" | `government-pitch.md` |
| **Economic Development Director** | Innovation metrics, business attraction, tax revenue | Shape strategy, allocate staff | "This competes with our existing programs" | `government-pitch.md` |
| **Innovation District Leadership** | Protecting existing investment, demonstrating relevance | Control physical district resources | "You're making us obsolete" | `institution-pitch.md` |
| **University President/Provost** | Research funding, student outcomes, prestige | Commit institutional resources | "What's in it for our researchers?" | `institution-pitch.md` |
| **Hospital/Health System CEO** | Community health outcomes, data strategy | Large employer influence | "We need data security guarantees" | `institution-pitch.md` |
| **Venture Capitalist** | Deal flow, portfolio company advantage, ROI | Fund or don't fund | "Where's the business model?" | `investor-pitch.md` |
| **Impact Investor** | Equitable outcomes, ESG metrics, scalable models | Patient capital | "How do you measure impact?" | `investor-pitch.md` |
| **Small Business Owner (corridor)** | More customers, lower costs, simpler operations | Adopt or ignore tools | "I don't need more technology" | `community-pitch.md` |
| **Resident (target neighborhoods)** | Jobs, opportunity, not getting displaced | Vote, participate, advocate | "Is this going to gentrify my neighborhood?" | `community-pitch.md` |
| **Community Organization Leader** | Serving constituents, access to resources | Mobilize community | "Who actually benefits from this?" | `community-pitch.md` |
| **Journalist / Media** | Compelling story, national significance, data | Amplify or ignore | "How is this different from every other 'smart city' thing?" | `media-pitch.md` |
| **Foundation Program Officer** | Scalable impact, measurable outcomes, replicability | Grant decisions | "What's the sustainability plan after grant funding?" | `investor-pitch.md` |
| **Federal Agency (EDA, SBA, HUD)** | Scalable model, equity, economic resilience | Grant funding | "Is this proven or theoretical?" | `government-pitch.md` |

## Messaging Principles

1. **Lead with outcomes, not technology.** Nobody cares about APIs. They care about jobs, businesses, and opportunity.
2. **Acknowledge what exists.** Never position the Digital District as a replacement for Cortex/39 North. Position it as the operating system that extends their reach.
3. **Use the audience's language.** Government: "economic resilience." Investors: "deal flow and ROI." Community: "access and opportunity."
4. **One number per audience.** Give each audience a single number they can repeat: "$4.3M cost vs. $1.3B for the old model" or "500 new businesses in Year 1."
5. **Address the objection before they raise it.** If you know the Innovation District leader thinks you're making them obsolete, say "Cortex becomes more valuable, not less" in your first sentence.
