# Partner Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) Template

## MEMORANDUM OF UNDERSTANDING

**Between:** [DIGITAL DISTRICT ENTITY NAME] ("District")
**And:** [PARTNER ORGANIZATION NAME] ("Partner")
**Effective Date:** [DATE]
**Duration:** [DURATION] with option to renew

### 1. Purpose

This MOU establishes the framework for collaboration between the District and Partner in deploying and operating Digital District infrastructure on [CORRIDOR/AREA NAME].

### 2. Shared Objectives

Both parties commit to:
- Expanding equitable access to digital tools and infrastructure
- Supporting entrepreneurship across all neighborhoods
- Maintaining transparent governance and public accountability
- Protecting user privacy and data rights
- Contributing to the open-source Digital District ecosystem

### 3. District Responsibilities

The District agrees to:
- Deploy and maintain Digital District infrastructure (Layers 1-4) on [CORRIDOR]
- Provide Partner with API access to District services
- Include Partner representation in governance structure
- Share aggregated, anonymized ecosystem metrics with Partner
- Acknowledge Partner's contribution in public communications
- [ADDITIONAL SPECIFIC COMMITMENTS]

### 4. Partner Responsibilities

The Partner agrees to:
- [SPECIFIC CONTRIBUTION: e.g., "Provide community Wi-Fi backhaul from existing fiber infrastructure"]
- [SPECIFIC CONTRIBUTION: e.g., "Assign 2 staff members to community engagement efforts"]
- [SPECIFIC CONTRIBUTION: e.g., "Open institutional APIs for integration with District services"]
- [SPECIFIC CONTRIBUTION: e.g., "Contribute $X to Phase 1 deployment"]
- Participate in [WORKING GROUP NAME] working group
- [ADDITIONAL SPECIFIC COMMITMENTS]

### 5. Governance

- Partner will hold [NUMBER] seat(s) on the [GOVERNING BODY]
- Partner acknowledges the Community Advisory Board's veto authority on data governance and equity policies
- Decision-making follows the RACI matrix published in the governance model

### 6. Data Governance

- User data is owned by users, not by either party
- Data sharing between District and Partner follows the Data Sharing Framework (Level [X] classification)
- A Data Sharing Agreement (DSA) will be executed as a separate document within 60 days
- Neither party will sell, share, or monetize user data without explicit user consent

### 7. Intellectual Property

- All software developed under this MOU is released under MIT License
- Neither party claims proprietary rights over collaboratively developed tools
- Pre-existing IP remains with the originating party
- Partner grants District a non-exclusive license to use Partner's name and logo in Digital District materials

### 8. Financial Terms

- [DESCRIBE FINANCIAL COMMITMENTS FROM EACH PARTY]
- [DESCRIBE COST-SHARING ARRANGEMENTS IF ANY]
- [DESCRIBE IN-KIND CONTRIBUTIONS IF ANY]

### 9. Term and Termination

- This MOU is effective for [DURATION] from the Effective Date
- Either party may terminate with 90 days written notice
- Upon termination: shared infrastructure transitions per a migration plan agreed within 30 days of notice
- User data and access are not disrupted by partner termination

### 10. Dispute Resolution

- Disputes resolved first through direct conversation between designated representatives
- If unresolved within 30 days, escalated to the DD Authority Board for mediation
- If still unresolved, parties agree to binding arbitration under [JURISDICTION] rules

### 11. Not a Binding Contract

This MOU expresses the intent of both parties to collaborate. It is not a legally binding contract. Binding obligations (data sharing, financial commitments, IP licenses) will be established in separate agreements referenced herein.
