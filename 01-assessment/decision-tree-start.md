# Decision Tree: Should You Build a Digital District?

## Entry Point

**Question 1: Does your city/region have an existing Innovation District?**

- **YES** → Go to Question 2
- **NO** → Go to Question 5

---

### Question 2: Is the Innovation District delivering equitable economic outcomes?

*Criteria: Are entrepreneurs from all neighborhoods, demographics, and income levels benefiting — or only those within/near the district?*

- **YES, equitable outcomes** → Your Innovation District is working. Consider adding a digital layer to amplify it. Go to **Path A: Augment**
- **NO, concentrated benefits** → The physical model has structural limits. Go to Question 3

### Question 3: Is there political will to invest in digital infrastructure alongside (not replacing) the physical district?

- **YES** → Go to **Path B: Hybrid**
- **NO** → Go to Question 4

### Question 4: Is there a cultural/commercial corridor that could serve as a pilot Digital Mainstreet?

*Criteria: A corridor with existing foot traffic, cultural identity, transit access, and community trust — but underserved by the Innovation District.*

- **YES** → Go to **Path C: Corridor Pilot**
- **NO** → Go to **Path D: Virtual-First**

### Question 5: Does your city have a cultural/commercial corridor with strong community identity?

- **YES** → Go to **Path C: Corridor Pilot**
- **NO** → Go to Question 6

### Question 6: Does your city have cloud infrastructure, AI talent, or a tech community — even if small?

- **YES** → Go to **Path D: Virtual-First**
- **NO** → Go to **Path E: Foundation Building**

---

## Paths

### Path A: Augment the Existing District

**Situation:** You have a working Innovation District. You're not replacing it — you're extending its reach through digital infrastructure.

**Recommended actions:**
1. Run the Readiness Scorecard → identify gaps in Layers 2-4
2. Deploy AI intelligence layer (Layer 2) within the existing district first
3. Build APIs that expose district services to remote entrepreneurs (Layer 4)
4. Measure whether digital access increases participation from outside the district
5. If successful, expand digital layer citywide

**Timeline:** 6-12 months to digital augmentation

**Key risk:** The Innovation District governance structure resists digital expansion because it threatens the real-estate value proposition.

---

### Path B: Hybrid Model (Physical + Digital)

**Situation:** You have an Innovation District with concentrated benefits, and political support to invest in digital infrastructure alongside it.

**Recommended actions:**
1. Run the Readiness Scorecard → identify gaps across all layers
2. Designate the Innovation District as "Node 1" on the digital network
3. Deploy Layer 1 infrastructure on a pilot corridor *outside* the district
4. Build Layer 2 intelligence tools that serve both the district and the corridor
5. Connect them via shared APIs and identity (Layer 4)
6. Phase 3: Expand to citywide digital layer

**Timeline:** 12-24 months to operational hybrid

**Key risk:** Funding gets pulled from digital back to physical under political pressure. Secure multi-year commitments.

---

### Path C: Corridor Pilot (Digital Mainstreet)

**Situation:** You have a corridor with cultural identity and community trust, but no legacy Innovation District to work around (or the Innovation District isn't delivering).

**Recommended actions:**
1. Select the corridor using `03-decision-trees/corridor-selection.md`
2. Run the Readiness Scorecard specifically for the corridor
3. Deploy Layer 1 (community Wi-Fi, cloud infrastructure)
4. Deploy Layer 2 (AI tools for corridor businesses)
5. Deploy Layer 3 (applications for entrepreneurial workflows)
6. Document and open-source for replication

**Timeline:** 6-18 months to operational Digital Mainstreet

**Key risk:** Community perceives digital infrastructure as gentrification rather than inclusion. Community engagement (Scorecard Dimension 7) must lead technology deployment.

**This is the Delmar model.**

---

### Path D: Virtual-First Digital District

**Situation:** No strong physical corridor, but you have digital infrastructure and tech talent.

**Recommended actions:**
1. Build Layers 1-2 entirely in the cloud (no physical Wi-Fi deployment)
2. Create a virtual onboarding experience for entrepreneurs
3. Deploy Layer 3 applications accessible from anywhere
4. Use Layer 4 distribution to attract participants beyond your city
5. If demand grows, identify a physical corridor to overlay as the "home base"

**Timeline:** 3-9 months to operational virtual district

**Key risk:** Without physical grounding, the district may lack identity and community. Consider partnering with a community organization to provide the social layer.

---

### Path E: Foundation Building

**Situation:** No Innovation District, no strong corridor, limited tech infrastructure. You're starting from scratch.

**Recommended actions:**
1. Build the case — use `04-messaging/` to develop stakeholder pitches
2. Identify 2-3 champions (city council, university, community org)
3. Secure seed funding for a feasibility study
4. Run the Readiness Scorecard to establish a baseline
5. Pick the lowest-cost, highest-impact starting point from the gap analysis
6. Build a coalition before building technology

**Timeline:** 12-24 months to pilot-ready

**Key risk:** Trying to build technology before building the coalition. Technology without community is an empty shell.

---

## Decision Summary

| Path | Starting Point | First Action | Timeline |
|------|---------------|--------------|----------|
| A: Augment | Working Innovation District | Deploy AI layer within district | 6-12 months |
| B: Hybrid | Innovation District + political will | Designate district as Node 1, pilot corridor outside | 12-24 months |
| C: Corridor Pilot | Strong cultural corridor | Deploy community Wi-Fi + AI tools on corridor | 6-18 months |
| D: Virtual-First | Tech talent, no physical corridor | Build cloud-native ecosystem | 3-9 months |
| E: Foundation | Starting from scratch | Build coalition and case | 12-24 months |
