# Decision Tree: Selecting a Digital Mainstreet Corridor

## Purpose

Choose the right physical corridor for a Digital Mainstreet deployment. The wrong corridor produces an empty technical exercise. The right corridor produces a thriving ecosystem.

## Evaluation Criteria

Score each candidate corridor 1-5 on these seven dimensions:

### 1. Cultural Identity (Weight: 20%)

*Does this corridor have a name, a story, and a community that identifies with it?*

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 1 | No identity — generic commercial strip |
| 2 | Some local recognition but no cultural significance |
| 3 | Known locally, some cultural programming |
| 4 | Strong cultural identity, regular events, media coverage |
| 5 | Nationally recognized cultural corridor with deep community roots |

### 2. Transit Connectivity (Weight: 15%)

*Can people get there without a car?*

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 1 | Car-dependent, no transit |
| 2 | Infrequent bus service only |
| 3 | Regular bus service, some bike infrastructure |
| 4 | Multi-modal (bus + rail or BRT), good pedestrian access |
| 5 | High-frequency multi-modal transit hub |

### 3. Existing Business Density (Weight: 15%)

*Are there businesses already operating that can benefit immediately?*

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 1 | < 10 active businesses |
| 2 | 10-25 active businesses |
| 3 | 25-50 active businesses |
| 4 | 50-100 active businesses |
| 5 | 100+ active businesses with diverse categories |

### 4. Socioeconomic Diversity (Weight: 20%)

*Does this corridor bridge different communities, especially underserved ones?*

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 1 | Serves a single, already-advantaged demographic |
| 2 | Low diversity, limited connection to underserved areas |
| 3 | Some diversity, adjacent to different income areas |
| 4 | Sits between distinct communities, serves as a bridge |
| 5 | Directly bridges a socioeconomic divide with deep community on both sides |

### 5. Physical Suitability (Weight: 10%)

*Can Wi-Fi mesh nodes be deployed? Is there power, line-of-sight, mounting infrastructure?*

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 1 | Hostile environment — no mounting points, extreme distances |
| 2 | Difficult — scattered buildings, poor line-of-sight |
| 3 | Workable with significant engineering |
| 4 | Good — regular building spacing, available mounting points |
| 5 | Ideal — dense, regular structure, existing utility infrastructure |

### 6. Community Trust (Weight: 15%)

*Will the community welcome this, or resist it?*

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 1 | Active distrust of institutional projects |
| 2 | Skepticism, history of broken promises |
| 3 | Neutral, willing to listen |
| 4 | Positive, existing community organizations ready to partner |
| 5 | Enthusiastic, community organizations actively requesting digital infrastructure |

### 7. Strategic Significance (Weight: 5%)

*Does this corridor tell a compelling story for media, funders, and other cities?*

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 1 | No strategic narrative |
| 2 | Locally interesting |
| 3 | Regionally significant |
| 4 | Nationally noteworthy |
| 5 | Iconic — deploying here makes national news |

## Scoring

```
Weighted Score = (Cultural × 0.20) + (Transit × 0.15) + (Business × 0.15) + 
                 (Diversity × 0.20) + (Physical × 0.10) + (Trust × 0.15) + 
                 (Strategic × 0.05)
```

| Score Range | Recommendation |
|-------------|---------------|
| 4.0 - 5.0 | Strong candidate — proceed to community engagement |
| 3.0 - 3.9 | Viable with investment — identify and address weak dimensions |
| 2.0 - 2.9 | Risky — consider a different corridor or address fundamentals first |
| < 2.0 | Not suitable — choose a different corridor |

## Example: Delmar Boulevard / The Loop (St. Louis)

| Dimension | Score | Rationale |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Cultural Identity | 5 | Nationally known cultural corridor, Walk of Fame, entertainment district |
| Transit Connectivity | 4 | MetroLink station, multiple bus routes, bike lane, walkable |
| Business Density | 4 | 100+ businesses including restaurants, retail, entertainment |
| Socioeconomic Diversity | 5 | Sits directly on the Delmar Divide — the most documented socioeconomic line in America |
| Physical Suitability | 4 | Dense commercial strip, regular building spacing, existing utility poles |
| Community Trust | 3 | Mixed — deep community roots but history of projects not delivering for north side |
| Strategic Significance | 5 | "Bridging the Delmar Divide with digital infrastructure" is a national story |

**Weighted Score: 4.35 — Strong candidate**

**Weakest dimension: Community Trust (3).** This must be addressed through Phase 1 community engagement before technology deployment. The community engagement workstream is not optional — it's the prerequisite.

## Disqualifying Factors

Regardless of score, do NOT select a corridor if:

- Active gentrification/displacement is occurring (digital overlay will be blamed)
- No community organization is willing to partner
- Physical infrastructure makes Wi-Fi deployment cost-prohibitive
- The corridor is scheduled for major construction in the next 18 months
- Institutional politics would prevent cross-sector collaboration
